Xenia Rubinos (born July 24, 1985) is an American singer, songwriter and multiinstrumentalist.

Xenia Rubinos was born in Hartford, CT in 1985 to a Puerto Rican mother and a Cuban father.

Her album Black Terry Cat was re- leased to critical acclaim and was named the 11th best album of 2016 by NPR.

Rubinos' music is not easily catego- rized, as she crosses many genres in both her lyrics and her sound

She is inspired by her Latin American heritage and Santería practices.

Xenia Rubinos is a New York-based artist, much appreciated for her innovative vocal style.

The singer and songwriters latest album is steeped in Caribbean texts that have influenced her musicianship.

In its shades of love and grief, the Album sounds like life as it arrives.

Una Rosa was inspired initially by the images and sounds that have stuck with Rubinos throughout her life.
